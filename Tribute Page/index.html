Pedro Paulet

Forerunner of propulsion air navigation and pioneer of the space age

A small sumary in the timeline of Pedro Paulet's life

  • 1874 - Born in Arequipa, Peru on July 2.
  • 1885 - Due to being orphaned of his father, he is welcomed by a religious to study at a Catholic school known as "the school of the poor".
    During his childhood he used to build rockets to play with them at carnivals and parties in his native Arequipa.
    Due to his excellent grades, he manages to obtain a scholarship granted by the Peruvian government to go to study in France.
  • 1901 - He obtained the title of chemical engineer, with the highest distinction, from the Sorbonne University, being admitted as a member of the Astronomical Society and the Chemical Society of Paris.
  • 1902 - He designs the torpedo-plane, the first antecedent of a rocket-powered ship.
  • 1905 - He returns to Peru to contribute to the development of his country after the disasters of the war with Chile.
    He is appointed director of the School of Arts and Crafts of Lima, inaugurated the same year.
  • 1905 - 1910 - Projects the construction of the Goyeneche hospital and a temple dedicated to Santa Rosa de Lima on the top of the San Cristobal hill.
  • 1907 - 1910 - He is in charge of directing the official newspaper "El Peruano" and the magazine "Ilustración Peruana" (1909 - 1910) where he disseminates technological advances.
  • 1911 - He resigns from working in the Peruvian state and returns to Europe. There he marries and engages in private affairs.
  • 1921 - He is convinced to return to the diplomatic service.
    He is credited as Peruvian consul in Dresden (1921), Amsterdam (1923), Christiania (or Oslo, 1924), and Rotterdam (1929).
  • 1928 - 1930 - He is mentioned in several European scientific articles highlighting him as the first to design a liquid propellant engine between 1895 and 1897.
  • 1930 - He leaves Europe and becomes consul in Yokohama, Japan, until 1934.
  • 1935 - Organizes the Commercial Department of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Peru, being director for 5 years.
  • 1935 - Publishes "Modern Japan and its Economic Foundations." A book that would anticipate the current Japanese economy and trade relations between the Pacific Rim countries.
    The Japanese emperor distinguished him with the Imperial Sword.
  • 1941 - He is commercial adviser to the Marshal and consul of Argentina, former president of Peru, Óscar R. Benavides.
  • 1944 - Proposes the creation of a Peruvian Aeronautical Industry.
  • 1945 - He died in Argentina on January 30.
    The Argentine government ordered that he be buried with the honors of Colonel of the Nation.
